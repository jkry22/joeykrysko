Goodwill Keystone Design Project

Goodwill Keystone sponsored a design challenge for my engineering design class. Students in every section attempted to solve one of Goodwill's main problems. My group decided to improve upon Goodwill's current Z-rack design by making it more spacially efficient. We also proposed an alternative to the current process which made use of the features on our improved rack.

At the Penn State Engineering Expo we presented our design to engineering professors and Goodwill Keystone Representatives, and we placed as finalists in the design challenge.

Within the group, I was primarily in charge of creating models of the new rack design using Solidworks. The images to the right show some of the models, and they also demonstrate how the rack can rotate for more efficient storage within Goodwill's warehouses and shipping trucks.
